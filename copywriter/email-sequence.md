## Initial Assessment

Before creating a sequence, understand:

1. **Sequence Type** — welcome, lead nurture, re-engagement, post-purchase, event-based, educational, sales, onboarding
2. **Audience** — who, what triggered entry, what they know/believe, current relationship
3. **Goals** — primary conversion goal, relationship goals, segmentation goals, success definition

## Core Principles

1. **One email, one job** — one primary purpose, one main CTA
2. **Value before ask** — lead with usefulness, build trust, earn the right to sell
3. **Relevance over volume** — fewer better emails win, segment for relevance
4. **Clear path forward** — every email moves them somewhere, next steps obvious

## Timing & Length

| Sequence | Emails | Spacing |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Welcome | 3-7 | Immediate, then 1-2 days |
| Lead nurture | 5-10 | 2-4 days apart |
| Onboarding | 5-10 | 1-3 days apart |
| Re-engagement | 3-5 | 2-5 days apart |
| Win-back | 2-4 | Spread over 30-90 days |

B2B: avoid weekends. B2C: test weekends. Send at local time.

## Subject Line Formulas

- **Question:** "Still struggling with X?"
- **How-to:** "How to [outcome] in [timeframe]"
- **Number:** "3 ways to [benefit]"
- **Direct:** "[First name], your [thing] is ready"
- **Story tease:** "The mistake I made with [topic]"

Rules: clear > clever, specific > vague, 40-60 chars, test emoji (polarizing)

**Preview text:** extends subject line, 90-140 chars, don't repeat subject, complete the thought or add intrigue.

## Awareness-Level Mapping

Match sequence strategy to prospect awareness (Schwartz scale):

| Awareness Level | Sequence Strategy | Email Tone |
|----------------|-------------------|------------|
| Unaware | Educational, problem-awareness content | Empathetic, storytelling |
| Problem-Aware | Agitate pain, introduce solution category | "You know the feeling..." |
| Solution-Aware | Differentiate your approach, methodology | Expert, comparative |
| Product-Aware | Overcome objections, case studies, proof | Confident, specific |
| Most Aware | Direct offer, urgency, risk reversal | Action-oriented, concise |

Sequence should progress awareness level by 1-2 stages over its duration.

## Welcome Sequence Template (Post-Signup)

**Email 1: Welcome (Immediate)**
- Subject: Welcome to [Product] -- here's your first step
- Deliver what was promised. Single next action. Set expectations.

**Email 2: Quick Win (Day 1-2)**
- Subject: Get your first [result] in 10 minutes
- Enable small success. Build confidence. Link to resource.

**Email 3: Story/Why (Day 3-4)**
- Subject: Why we built [Product]
- Origin story or mission. Emotional connection. Show you understand their problem.

**Email 4: Social Proof (Day 5-6)**
- Subject: How [Customer] achieved [Result]
- Case study or testimonial. Relatable. Soft CTA.

**Email 5: Overcome Objection (Day 7-8)**
- Subject: "I don't have time for X" -- sound familiar?
- Address common hesitation. Reframe obstacle. Show easy path.

**Email 6: Core Feature (Day 9-11)**
- Subject: Have you tried [Feature] yet?
- Highlight underused capability. Clear benefit. Direct CTA.

**Email 7: Conversion (Day 12-14)**
- Subject: Ready to [upgrade/buy/commit]?
- Summarize value. Clear offer. Urgency if appropriate. Risk reversal.

## Lead Nurture Sequence Template (Pre-Sale)

**Email 1: Deliver + Introduce (Immediate)** — deliver lead magnet, brief intro, preview what's coming
**Email 2: Expand (Day 2-3)** — related insight, establish expertise
**Email 3: Problem Deep-Dive (Day 4-5)** — articulate problem deeply, hint at solution
**Email 4: Solution Framework (Day 6-8)** — your methodology, educational not salesy
**Email 5: Case Study (Day 9-11)** — real results, specific and relatable
**Email 6: Differentiation (Day 12-14)** — why your approach is different, address alternatives
**Email 7: Objection Handler (Day 15-18)** — FAQ or myth-busting, reduce friction
**Email 8: Direct Offer (Day 19-21)** — clear pitch, strong value prop, specific CTA, urgency

## Re-Engagement Sequence Template

**Email 1: Check-In (Day 30-60 of inactivity)**
- Subject: Is everything okay, [Name]?
- Genuine concern. Ask what happened. Easy win to re-engage.

**Email 2: Value Reminder (Day 2-3 after)**
- Subject: Remember when you [achieved X]?
- Remind past value. What's new. Quick CTA.

**Email 3: Incentive (Day 5-7 after)**
- Subject: We miss you -- here's something special
- Offer if appropriate. Limited time. Clear CTA.

**Email 4: Last Chance (Day 10-14 after)**
- Subject: Should we stop emailing you?
- Honest and direct. One-click stay or go. Clean list if no response.

## Onboarding Sequence Template (Product Users)

**Email 1: Welcome + First Step (Immediate)** — confirm signup, one critical action, direct link
**Email 2: Getting Started (Day 1)** — if step 1 incomplete, quick tip/video, support option
**Email 3: Feature Highlight (Day 2-3)** — key feature, specific use case, in-app link
**Email 4: Success Story (Day 4-5)** — relatable customer journey, motivational
**Email 5: Check-In (Day 7)** — how's it going? ask for feedback, offer help
**Email 6: Advanced Tip (Day 10-12)** — power feature for engaged users
**Email 7: Upgrade/Expand (Day 14+)** — trial: conversion push, free: upgrade, paid: expansion

## Win-Back Templates

**Expired Trials (3-4 emails over 30 days):**
1. Day 1: what you're missing
2. Day 7: what held you back? (feedback)
3. Day 14: incentive (discount, extended trial)
4. Day 30: door is open

Segment by trial engagement: high = remove friction, low = offer fresh start, none = ask what happened.

**Cancelled Customers (2-3 emails over 90 days):**
1. Day 30: what's new since you left
2. Day 60: we addressed [common reason]
3. Day 90: special offer to return

## Lifecycle Email Types Reference

### Retention
- **Upgrade to paid** — trigger: engagement signals or trial ending. Value summary > feature comparison > social proof > urgency > final.
- **Upgrade to higher plan** — trigger: approaching limits. Acknowledge growth, show next tier value, quantify ROI.
- **Ask for review** — after positive support, milestone, or renewal. NOT after billing issues.
- **Proactive support** — trigger: usage drop, failed actions. Genuine concern, specific help, no sales pitch.
- **Usage report** — recurring. Key metrics, comparison, achievements, suggestions.
- **NPS survey** — quarterly. Promoters: ask for review/referral. Passives: what makes it 10? Detractors: personal outreach.
- **Referral program** — after positive NPS, achievement, or renewal. Easy sharing link. Show value for both sides.

### Billing
- **Switch to annual** — calculate exact savings, lock-in messaging, one-click switch.
- **Failed payment** — 4 emails over 10-14 days. Assume accident. Clear, direct, single CTA to update payment.
- **Cancellation survey** — immediate. Primary reason, what could improve, would anything change mind.
- **Renewal reminder** — 14-30 days before. Date, amount, what's included, how to update.

### Campaigns
- **Newsletter** — consistent schedule, scannable, mix content types, one primary CTA.
- **Seasonal promos** — announcement > reminder > last chance.
- **Product updates** — what's new, why it matters, how to use, who asked for it.
- **Pricing update** — 30-60 day advance notice. Honest, transparent, grandfather if possible.

## Email Copy Structure

1. **Hook** — first line grabs attention
2. **Context** — why this matters to them
3. **Value** — the useful content
4. **CTA** — what to do next
5. **Sign-off** — human, warm close

**Formatting:** short paragraphs (1-3 sentences), white space, bullets for scanability, bold sparingly, mobile-first.

**Length:** 50-125 words transactional, 150-300 educational, 300-500 story-driven. Shorter usually better.

**Tone:** conversational not formal, I/we and you, active voice, read aloud test.

## Segmentation

- **By behavior:** openers vs non-openers, clickers, active vs inactive
- **By stage:** trial vs paid, new vs long-term, engaged vs at-risk
- **By profile:** industry/role (B2B), use case/goal, company size

## Testing

Test one variable at a time: subject lines (highest impact), send times, length, CTA placement, personalization, timing.

**Benchmarks:** open rate 20-40%, click rate 2-5%, unsubscribe <0.5%.
