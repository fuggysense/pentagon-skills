### 2. Audience Targeting — Schwartz Awareness Framework

Map the target audience to their awareness stage. This determines ALL messaging decisions.

| Stage | Description | What They Need | Opening Strategy |
|-------|------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Unaware** | Don't know they have a problem | Agitate a latent pain | Lead with the problem (story, question, disruption) |
| **Problem-Aware** | Know the problem, not the solution | Name their pain precisely | Mirror their situation, validate the struggle |
| **Solution-Aware** | Know solutions exist, not yours | Differentiate your mechanism | Lead with your unique approach/mechanism |
| **Product-Aware** | Know your product, haven't bought | Overcome specific objections | Proof, risk reversal, urgency |
| **Most Aware** | Know and trust you, need a push | Remove final friction | Direct offer, exclusive deal, deadline |

**For this campaign:**
- Primary awareness stage: [____]
- Evidence: [How do you know they're at this stage?]
- Messaging approach: [What this means for headlines, hooks, CTAs]

### 3. Market Sophistication Stage

How many competing messages has this audience seen? Determines creative strategy.

| Level | Market State | Creative Strategy |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **L1** | First in market | Simple, direct claim. "We do X." |
| **L2** | Competitors exist, claims similar | Enlarge the claim. More, better, faster. Add specifics. |
| **L3** | Audience skeptical of claims | Introduce a unique MECHANISM. Name it. Explain HOW, not just what. |
| **L4** | Mechanisms copied too | Lead with IDENTIFICATION. Mirror the prospect. "You're the person who..." |
| **L5** | Total saturation | Speak as an insider. Break all ad conventions. Whisper, don't shout. |

**For this campaign:**
- Sophistication level: L[____]
- Evidence: [Competing messages seen, audience cynicism level]
- What to lead with: [____]
- What to NEVER lead with: [____]

### 4. Emotional Anchor Framework

Every campaign needs ONE dominant emotional driver. Secondary emotions support but don't compete.

**Select primary emotion:**

| Emotion | When to Use | Copy Signal |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Fear | Audience avoiding a negative outcome | "What happens if you don't..." |
| Aspiration | Audience pursuing a positive outcome | "Imagine when you..." |
| Frustration | Audience stuck, tried and failed | "Tired of... / You've tried X but..." |
| Shame | Audience feels behind peers | "Everyone else already..." (use carefully) |
| Relief | Audience overwhelmed, wants simplicity | "Finally, a way to... / No more..." |
| Curiosity | Audience doesn't know what they don't know | "The reason most people fail at..." |
| Belonging | Audience wants community/identity | "Join the [X] who..." |
| Vindication | Audience wants to prove doubters wrong | "They said you couldn't..." |

**Primary emotion:** [____]
**Secondary emotion:** [____]
**Buying emotion (deepest trigger):** Not "[surface desire]" but "[what actually causes action]"

### 5. Proof Requirements by Awareness Stage

Different stages trust different proof types:

| Stage | Proof That Works | Proof That Fails |
|-------|-----------------|------------------|
| Unaware | Stories, analogies, pattern interrupts | Data (they don't care yet) |
| Problem-Aware | "Someone like me" testimonials, pain validation | Feature lists, specs |
| Solution-Aware | Mechanism proof, process explanations, comparisons | Generic testimonials |
| Product-Aware | Specific case studies, ROI data, risk reversal | Vague social proof |
| Most Aware | Exclusive offers, scarcity, personal results | Hard sell (they already trust) |

### 7. Headline Direction Formulas

Match formula to awareness + sophistication:

**L1-L2 (Claim-led):**
- "[Specific result] in [timeframe]"
- "The [adjective] way to [desired outcome]"
- "[Number] [people/businesses] already [achieved result]"

**L3 (Mechanism-led):**
- "The [named mechanism] behind [result]"
- "Why [common approach] fails (and what works instead)"
- "How [specific process] produces [specific outcome]"

**L4 (Identification-led):**
- "You're the [type of person] who [specific behavior]"
- "If you've [tried X and felt Y], this is for you"
- "[Their exact internal monologue]"

**L5 (Insider-led):**
- Colloquial language, no ad conventions
- Peer-to-peer tone, not brand-to-consumer
- Ultra-specific references only insiders get

| **Anchoring** | Show higher price first, then actual |

## Content Mix Framework

- 70% proven content (what works, repeat)
- 20% iterative improvements (variations on winners)
- 10% experimental (new formats, topics, channels)

By type: 40% Educational, 30% Engaging, 20% Inspirational, 10% Promotional

## Anti-Patterns

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Same message to all awareness stages | Unaware don't care about features | Segment messaging by stage |
| Leading with features at L4-L5 | Audience is cynical, sees through claims | Lead with identification or insider tone |
| All BOFU content | Ignores 97% not ready to buy | Full-funnel approach |
| No proof elements | Claims without evidence = noise | Match proof type to awareness stage |
| Generic social proof | "Trusted by 10,000+" means nothing at L4 | Specific, named, verifiable proof |
| Shaming anxious buyers | Triggers defensiveness, not curiosity | Validate fears, then reframe |
| Quantity over quality | Dilutes brand, wastes resources | Better content, less often |
| Publish and forget | Wastes content investment | Promote and repurpose |
